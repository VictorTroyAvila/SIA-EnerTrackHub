Production and Exploration

This is an article about the upstream segment of the oil and
gas industry. It discusses exploration and production activities.
Exploration involves finding potential drilling sites using
geological and geophysical surveys. Production involves drilling
wells and extracting oil and gas. The two most important
technological advancements in production are horizontal
drilling and hydraulic fracturing. Horizontal drilling allows wells
to reach oil and gas deposits that were previously inaccessible.
Hydraulic fracturing is a technique that uses high pressure
liquid to create fractures in rock formations, which allows oil
and gas to flow more easily to the well. In the exploration
process, geologists and geophysicists use seismic surveys to
map the Earth's subsurface and identify potential oil and gas
reservoirs. Once a promising location is identified, a well is
drilled to test for the presence of hydrocarbons. If oil or gas is
found, the well is then completed and put into production.
