Top 5 Largest Companies Shaping the Global Energy Landscape

Oil and Gas Exploration

1. ExxonMobil

ExxonMobil is one of the largest publicly traded multinational oil and gas companies in the world. It is involved in various aspects of the energy industry, including exploration, production, refining, and distribution of petroleum products. The company operates across the entire spectrum of the oil and gas sector, from upstream activities such as discovering and extracting crude oil and natural gas to downstream activities involving refining, marketing, and distributing refined products like gasoline, diesel, and petrochemicals.

What they do

MATERIALS FOR MODERN LIVING

Supporting industry to reduce emissions.

DELIVERING INDUSTRIAL SOLUTIONS

Continuing to reduce emissions today and into the future.

LOWER-EMISSION TRANSPORTATION

Creating innovative solutions that make modern life possible..

Projects

ExxonMobil, a global energy leader, extends its reach beyond fossil fuels with pioneering technological projects across the globe. From harnessing the wind's power in Scotland's Westray Wind Farm to exploring solar integration in Germany's Altona Project, the company showcases its commitment to cleaner energy solutions. Collaborations with research institutions and investments in renewable energy startups solidify its dedication to driving innovation in this field. While the scale of these projects compared to traditional operations remains smaller, ExxonMobil's steps towards a diversified energy portfolio mark a significant shift in the industry's landscape.

2. Shell Global

Royal Dutch Shell, is a global energy company engaged in various aspects of the energy industry. It is one of the largest publicly traded oil and gas companies in the world. Shell operates in different segments of the energy sector, including exploration and production, refining, distribution, and marketing of oil and gas products. The company is involved in activities such as discovering and extracting crude oil and natural gas, refining petroleum products like gasoline, diesel, and petrochemicals, and distributing these products to customers worldwide through its extensive network of retail stations.

Major Projects

Shell Polymers Monaca

In November 2022, Shell Chemical Appalachia LLC a subsidiary of Shell plc, commenced operations at its Shell Polymers Monaca (SPM) site, previously referred to as the Pennsylvania Chemical project. This world-class facility uses low-cost ethane from shale gas to produce polyethylene.

LNG Canada

LNG Canada is a major liquefied natural gas (LNG) project currently under construction on the west coast of Canada. It is expected to deliver its first LNG shipment before the middle of the next decade.

PRELUDE FLNG

The Prelude floating liquefied natural gas (FLNG) facility produces natural gas off the coast of Australia. In June 2019, it reached a significant milestone, shipping its first liquefied natural gas cargo to customers in Asia.

4. NextEra Energy

NextEra Energy, Inc. (NYSE: NEE) is a leading clean energy company headquartered in Juno Beach, Florida. NextEra Energy owns Florida Power & Light Company, which is America’s largest electric utility that sells more power than any other utility, providing clean, affordable, reliable electricity to approximately 5.8 million customer accounts, or more than 12 million people across Florida. NextEra Energy also owns a competitive clean energy business, NextEra Energy Resources, LLC, which, together with its affiliated entities, is the world’s largest generator of renewable energy from the wind and sun and a world leader in battery storage. Through its subsidiaries, NextEra Energy generates clean, emissions-free electricity from seven commercial nuclear power units in Florida, New Hampshire and Wisconsin.

5. Duke Energy Corporation

Duke Energy is one of the largest electric power holding companies in the United States, serving approximately 7.8 million customers across several states. It operates in two primary segments: regulated utilities and commercial renewable energy. In the regulated utilities segment, Duke Energy delivers electricity to customers in the Carolinas, Florida, Ohio, Kentucky, and Indiana. The company generates electricity through various sources, including nuclear, coal, natural gas, and hydroelectric power plants. Duke Energy Renewables focuses on developing, constructing, and operating wind and solar renewable generation facilities across the country. It has a significant portfolio of renewable energy assets, contributing to the company's commitment to transitioning towards cleaner energy sources.

Major Projects

Modernization and Grid Improvement Initiatives

Duke Energy has undertaken initiatives aimed at modernizing its energy infrastructure and enhancing grid reliability.

Solar Power Projects

Duke Energy has been actively investing in solar energy projects across several states in the United States.

Carbon Emission Reduction Programs

Duke Energy has been working on programs to reduce its carbon footprint and transition towards cleaner energy sources.
